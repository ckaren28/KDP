## Concept

Traveler began with a suitcase and a question: what do you keep when you have to leave everything behind?

Designed during a period of personal transition. Moving cities, living without a fixed address, existing between places. This collection explores modular dressing as a philosophy rather than a gimmick. Each piece is designed to pack flat, layer intuitively, and shift between contexts: a dinner, a flight, a meeting, a morning walk in an unfamiliar city.

The woman this collection is for knows how to travel light without looking like it. She moves through the world with intention. Her wardrobe doesn't announce itself.

**Principle 01 — Pack flat.** Every silhouette considered for the suitcase first.

**Principle 02 — Wear twice.** Each piece designed to function across at least two distinct contexts.

**Principle 03 — Arrive ready.** No piece that requires ironing, steaming, or special care on the road.

## Technical Development

Developed through mood research, hand sketching, and CAD technical flat work. Fabric selection prioritized wrinkle resistance, packability, and a restrained palette that travels well across climates and occasions. The collection was designed to be finished in construction. The next phase would be full pattern development and sample making.
