## Construction

A tailored blazer is a study in structure: the silhouette doesn't exist in the fabric itself but in what's built underneath it. Shoulder pads and sleeve heads push the shoulder into shape. Darts take up the ease at the waist. The full lining conceals all of it and makes the inside as considered as the outside.

Welt pockets are among the most technically demanding pocket types — a clean, finished slot in the face fabric with no visible stitching from the front.
